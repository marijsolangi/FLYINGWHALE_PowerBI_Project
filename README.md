# Flying Whale Airline Case Study Report

This repository contains a comprehensive data analysis report for Flying Whale Airline, authored by Muhammad Marij.[cite: 2] 

## Introduction

Flying Whale Airline, a leading international airline, is currently facing challenges in maintaining customer loyalty and optimizing its loyalty programs.[cite: 2] Despite a growing customer base, shifting customer behavior and an increasing number of cancellations are raising concerns.[cite: 2] The airline aims to strengthen its business intelligence by analyzing Customer Flight Activity and Loyalty History to enhance the customer experience, improve retention, and better understand loyalty trends.[cite: 2]

## Data Overview and Cleaning

The data for this analysis spans the years 2017 and 2018 and was sourced from two CSV files on GitHub.[cite: 2] Key variables analyzed include Loyalty Number as a unique identifier, Demographics (country, province, gender, education, salary, and marital status), Enrollment and Cancellation Details, Flight Activity, Loyalty Card Tier (Aurora, Nova, and Star), and Customer Lifetime Value (CLV).[cite: 2]

To ensure accuracy, the data cleaning process involved removing over 1,000 duplicate entries from the Flight Activity CSV.[cite: 2] The process also included correcting data types for proper analysis and creating new columns, such as Enrollment Duration and Cancellation Month Number, to enhance insights into customer behavior.[cite: 2]

## Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

* A total of 16.74k customers have enrolled and flown with Flying Whale Airlines.[cite: 2]
* The airline has covered a total distance of 490 million kilometers.[cite: 2]
* Over 2 million flights have been booked with Flying Whale.[cite: 2]
* The average Customer Lifetime Value (CLV) stands at 7.99k Canadian dollars.[cite: 2]

## Flight Activity Insights

* **Booking Patterns:** From 2017 to 2018, flight bookings displayed a clear upward trend with notable seasonal fluctuations, recording the highest bookings in December 2018 and the lowest in January 2017.[cite: 2]
* **Companion Bookings:** Members redeem the most points when there are zero companions, indicating a negative correlation between companion bookings and loyalty points redeemed.[cite: 2]
* **Flight Distance:** There is a clear positive correlation between flight distance and loyalty points earned, with the highest point accumulation occurring within the 3,000 to 4,000 km range.[cite: 2]

## Loyalty Segmentation and Demographics

* **Card Tiers:** Star cardholders demonstrate the highest total number of flights, Nova cardholders show moderate activity, and Aurora cardholders have the lowest total number of flights.[cite: 2]
* **Peak Activity:** In July, the highest number of flights was recorded for all three tiers.[cite: 2]
* **Marital Status:** Most of the customers are married, and the majority of these married customers hold Star loyalty cards.[cite: 2]
* **Customer Lifetime Value (CLV):** The Average Customer Lifetime Value (CLV) decreases as the loyalty card tier increases.[cite: 2] Aurora tier customers have the highest CLV, followed by Nova, and then Star.[cite: 2]
* **Distance Traveled:** Aurora cardholders travel the farthest with a median distance of 543 km, followed by Star members (536 km) and Nova members (491 km).[cite: 2]
* **Gender Distribution:** The distribution of flights booked is relatively balanced across different loyalty card tiers, showing only minor variations between male and female passengers.[cite: 2]

## Enrollment and Cancellation Trends

* **Geographic Cancellations:** British Columbia, Ontario, and Quebec are the provinces with the fastest member cancellations.[cite: 2]
* **Seasonal Cancellations:** Cancellations peak in December, June, and August, while April, May, and February experienced the lowest cancellation activity.[cite: 2]
* **Demographic Cancellations:** Married individuals holding a Bachelor's degree have the highest cancellation count (865 cancellations), followed by single individuals with a College degree (290 cancellations).[cite: 2]
* **Enrollment Duration:** Among canceled members, Aurora cardholders have the shortest enrollment duration (3.4K months), Nova cardholders show moderate commitment (5.7K months), and Star cardholders have the longest enrollment duration (7.6K months).[cite: 2]

## Strategic Recommendations

Based on the case study findings, the following actions are proposed to enhance customer loyalty and optimize programs:[cite: 2]

* **Targeted Loyalty Programs:** Develop tailored retention strategies and personalized offers, specifically targeting Aurora cardholders who demonstrate a tendency to cancel sooner.[cite: 2]
* **Customer Feedback Mechanism:** Implement surveys or focus groups to understand the reasons behind cancellations and address customer pain points.[cite: 2]
* **Enhanced Marketing Strategies:** Utilize demographic data to focus promotional efforts on family-oriented travel packages for married individuals with Bachelor's degrees, as they exhibit high cancellation rates.[cite: 2]
* **Seasonal Retention Strategies:** Deploy promotions, special events, and loyalty bonuses during the high-cancellation months of December, June, and August.[cite: 2]
* **Improve Enrollment Duration:** Investigate factors contributing to short enrollment durations and offer loyalty bonuses or grace periods that reward longer engagement.[cite: 2]
* **Companion Booking Incentives:** Develop incentives for companion bookings to balance their effect on points redemption and encourage members to travel with companions.[cite: 2]
* **Flight Distance Rewards:** Offer bonus points or rewards for longer flights to motivate customers to choose Flying Whale Airline over competitors for extended trips.[cite: 2]
* **Data-Driven Decision Making:** Continuously analyze customer flight activity and loyalty history using advanced analytics to identify emerging trends.[cite: 2]
* **Customer Education:** Increase awareness of loyalty program benefits and educate customers on how to maximize points through frequent flying.[cite: 2]
